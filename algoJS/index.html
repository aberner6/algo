From the Outside In considers the perceptron: an algorithm that describes how our neurons process our environment - full of smell, touch, sight, sound and taste.
If we find something significant in all of these incoming senses, our neurons will try to pass a signal along to its network, communicating that we should pay attention to that smell - a whiff of smoke coming from down the hall, for example.

- 2 input neurons
- Linked through weighted lines
- To an output neuron.

Try triggering these sense inputs: see if the output neuron responds

The output neuron has a threshold (set randomly at 0.5 here) that the input multiplied by the weight of its connection must exceed:
input*weight>threshold
